# Detecting-Bias-in-Indian-Educational-Content-

Visit https://blockcrypts.com/


Educational content shapes how students understand the world, their place in it, and what futures are possible for them
Research Question
Can computational methods detect and quantify representational and linguistic bias in Indian educational content across subjects, grades, and content sources?

Motivation

Educational content shapes how students understand the world, their place in it, and what futures are possible for them. When textbooks consistently depict women in domestic roles, show only urban settings as "normal," or use upper-caste names in success stories, these patterns accumulate into a measurable, harmful bias — even if no single example is overtly discriminatory.

This project applies data science and natural language processing to measure these patterns at scale across Indian school-level educational content.

---

## Sub-Questions

1. Are male and female characters represented unequally in examples, stories, and professions?
2. Are certain professions disproportionately associated with one gender?
3. Are urban contexts over-represented relative to rural contexts?
4. Are some regions, cultures, or languages underrepresented?
5. Does AI-generated educational content reproduce or amplify these patterns?
6. Can we quantify these differences with interpretable metrics?
